Welcome To Bentota Turtle Hatchery



Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a turtle conservation project located in the coastal town of Bentota in Sri Lanka. It focuses on protecting sea turtle eggs and hatchlings, providing care and rehabilitation for injured or sick turtles, raising public awareness, and organizing turtle release programs. It also collaborates with local and international organizations to conduct research on sea turtles, contributing to the overall knowledge of sea turtle ecology and aiding in implementing effective conservation strategies. The hatchery serves as an important conservation hub, where efforts are made to protect and conserve sea turtles.

Following are some details regarding Kasgoda Turtle Hatchery



Turtle Conservation Efforts




Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation organization that protects turtle nests, incubates eggs, releases hatchlings, and engages with the local community to promote turtle conservation. These efforts include nest protection, incubation and hatching, predator control, release programs, community involvement, research and monitoring, and outreach programs. These efforts are essential for the long-term survival of sea turtles, as they protect nests, incubate eggs, release hatchlings, and involve the community.



Turtle Species




Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is home to four turtle species: Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas), Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta), Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea), Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata), and Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea). Green Turtles are known for their herbivorous diet, while Loggerhead Turtles are known for their large heads and strong jaws. Olive Ridley Turtles are known for their synchronized nesting behavior, while Hawksbill Turtles are critically endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and other human activities. Conservation efforts at Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery play a crucial role in protecting these species and raising awareness about their conservation needs.
